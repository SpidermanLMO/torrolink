# ROADBLOCK — PTorro Digital Pitch Playbook
### Sales Scripts + Objection Handling
_For Beachhead field ops — walk-in cold sales_

---

## The Setup

**Before you walk in:**
- You have a mock site already built for this specific business (Snake Eyes built it)
- You have the URL pulled up on your laptop or tablet — ready to show
- You know the business name, what they do, roughly what jobs are worth

**Rule:** Never pitch without the site already built. The site IS the pitch.

---

## The Walk-In (Opening Line)

> "Hey, I don't want to take much of your time — I'm Beachhead with PTorro Digital. I noticed you guys don't have a website, so I built one for you. Can I show you? Takes 30 seconds."

**Variants by situation:**

If they have a bad website:
> "Hey, I work with local businesses on their web presence. I took a look at your site and honestly — you deserve better. I rebuilt it. Can I show you real quick?"

If owner isn't there:
> "Is the owner around? I want to show them something — takes 2 minutes, no pitch, just want to show you what we built."

If they seem busy:
> "I'll be fast — I built your business a website. I just want to show you the homepage. 20 seconds."

---

## The 30-Second Demo

**Step 1 — Hand them the tablet or turn the laptop:**
> "This is your site. We put your name, your services, your phone number — click-to-call right there. Mobile. Fast. Clean."

**Step 2 — Scroll slowly. Point to:**
- Their business name at the top
- The click-to-call button
- The services section
- The lead form

> "Anyone who finds you on Google can reach you right here. You get an email the second they fill this out."

**Step 3 — The close:**
> "We do this for $529.28 — one time. No monthly fees unless you want us keeping it updated. For what you charge per job, this pays for itself in one call."

**Pause. Let them respond.**

## The 5 Objections — Word for Word

### 1. "I already have a website."

> "Can I see it real quick?"

*(Pull it up on your phone. If it's bad:)*
> "So this is what customers see when they find you on Google. How many calls do you think this gets you? Our sites are mobile-first, load in under 2 seconds, and have a lead form built in. Would you want to compare side-by-side?"

*(If it's actually decent:)*
> "That's actually solid. You're ahead of most businesses around here. We specialize in trades and services — if you ever need a redesign or a second site for a different service area, keep us in mind." *(Leave a card. Move on.)*

---

### 2. "I can't afford it."

> "I hear you. What do you charge for a [job]?"

*(Wait for answer — usually $200–$2,000+)*

> "So one call from this site covers it. You're not paying $529 — you're investing it to get your next 10 customers. And if you're slow right now, that's exactly when you need this most."

*(If they're still hesitant:)*
> "We also do a payment plan — $265 today, $265 when the site goes live. I can set that up right now."

---

### 3. "I'll think about it."

> "Totally fair. What's the main thing you want to think through?"

*(Listen. Address that specific thing.)*

> "Here's the thing — I have this site built for you today. If I leave and you decide in two weeks, I'll have moved on and built it for someone else in the area. I can hold it for you for 48 hours if you want to talk it over."

*(Pull up the Stripe payment link:)*
> "Or if you want to lock it in right now, I can take a deposit of $265 — no risk, you can get a refund in 72 hours if you change your mind."

---

### 4. "My nephew/friend does websites."

> "That's great — are they doing it for you? Or is it one of those things that's been 'coming soon' for a while?"

*(Pause — let them answer.)*

> "What we do is different — I walk in with it already built, it goes live in 48 hours, and you get ongoing support. Family's great but deadlines aren't exactly their specialty."

*(If the nephew actually built it and it's decent: walk away gracefully.)*

---

### 5. "I use Facebook / Yelp / Google Maps."

> "Those are great for existing customers finding you. But when someone searches 'plumber near me' at 11pm — Google shows websites, not Facebook pages. You want to show up there too."

> "Also — you don't own your Facebook page. They can change the algorithm, charge you for ads, or shut it down tomorrow. A website is yours forever."

---

## Closing Lines

**Hard close:**
> "You ready to get this live? I can have it up by Thursday."

**Soft close:**
> "What would it take for this to make sense for you today?"

**Walk-away close (if they're dragging):**
> "No pressure at all. Here's my card — when you're ready, the site is ready. But I can't hold it forever."

---

## After the Sale

1. Collect payment via Stripe link (send to their phone on the spot)
2. Get their email for the receipt
3. Confirm: "I'll have the final version to you within 48 hours. Any changes — hours, photos, contact info — send them to me and Snake Eyes will update it before we go live."
4. Log the sale in memory/web-design-tracker.md
