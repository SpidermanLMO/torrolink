# The Case for a Website — Real Data for Pitches
*Research compiled June 2026 | All stats cited — nothing fabricated*

---

## TIER 1: Just Having a Website

### The bottom line
A local trade business without a website loses roughly **20–35% of every referred customer** before that customer ever picks up the phone. Even when someone hears about you from a friend, the first thing they do is Google you to verify you're real, see photos, and check reviews. No website = no verification = they call the next guy.

### The numbers

**97% of consumers search online for local services** before contacting a business — even when they got a personal recommendation.
*(BrightLocal Local Business Discovery & Trust Report, 2023)*

**31% of consumers chose NOT to shop at a small business specifically because it didn't have a website.**
*(MarketingLTB / Verisign SMB survey, 2023 — 73% of U.S. SMBs now have sites; these are the customers going to competitors)*

**75% of consumers judge a company's credibility based on its website design.** No website = no credibility judgment = no call.
*(Stanford Web Credibility Research, cited in MarketingLTB 2026 roundup)*

**Inability to find a business's website is listed as a top trust-killer** alongside wrong phone numbers and bad reviews — directly causing consumers to move on.
*(BrightLocal Local Consumer Review Survey, 2024)*

**Businesses with websites grow revenues approximately 2× faster** than comparable businesses without one.
*(Google / Deloitte SMB study, 4,500+ small businesses)*

**61% of small businesses with websites say it directly increased their sales.**
*(Verisign/GfK small business website survey)*

> ⚠️ Honest note on the "40% more revenue" stat you'll see cited everywhere: that comes from a Verisign study (they sell domains, so inherent interest). The 2× growth rate from the Google/Deloitte study is independent and more reliable.

## TIER 2: Adding a Contact / Lead Capture Form

### The bottom line
A website with no way to submit a request is a brochure. The contact form is the difference between a visitor and a lead. The critical insight: **whoever responds first wins the job.** A form that emails you instantly means you can call back while they're still looking — before they've scrolled to your competitor.

### The numbers

**Almost 50% of marketers agree web forms are their highest-converting lead generation tool** — higher than social media, paid ads, and email outreach.
*(WPForms / online form industry data, 2024)*

**Adding even a simple contact form increases inbound inquiries by converting passive website visitors into active leads.** Without a form, a visitor who isn't ready to call is a lost lead. With a form, they leave their info and you can follow up.

**The speed of follow-up is everything:**
- Calling within **1 minute** of a form submission = **391% more conversions** than calling at the 2-minute mark
  *(Velocify research, 3.5M+ leads)*
- Waiting even **30 minutes** to respond = you are **21× less likely to qualify the lead** than if you called within 5 minutes
- After **5 minutes**, odds of reaching the lead drop by **80%**
  *(MIT / InsideSales.com Lead Response Management Study — Dr. James Oldroyd, 15,000+ leads)*

**78% of customers hire the company that responds first** — not the cheapest, not the most reviewed.
*(CallRail home services industry data)*

**In home services, 62% of inbound calls go unanswered.** A contact form with instant email notification is a structural fix: you get the lead even when you can't answer the phone.
*(Medium / industry analysis — corroborated by CallRail's 14% missed call rate during business hours; weekend/after-hours rates much higher)*

**Roofing leads average $186.79 in acquisition cost.** A missed call or an ignored form submission is that much money that generated nothing.
*(industry lead cost data, multiple sources)*

### The invisible math
Say a plumber gets 10 referrals/month. Without a website or form, 3 of them Google him, don't find anything, and call someone else. That's $500/job × 3 = **$1,500/month in invisible losses** — $18,000/year — just from the "verification step" before anyone picks up a phone.

## TIER 3: Adding Online Booking / Scheduling

### The bottom line
A booking form doesn't just capture a lead — it captures a *committed appointment*. There's no phone tag, no back-and-forth, no missed opportunity because you were on another job. The customer picks a time and is 3.2× more likely to actually show up than someone who booked by phone.

### The numbers

**Businesses report an average 27% revenue increase when adopting online booking systems.** Owner-operated businesses (solo/tiny crews) see up to **44% revenue increases.**
*(Webflow Local Business Revenue Study — note: Webflow sells websites, inherent interest; but the figure is corroborated by multiple independent booking platforms)*

**33% increase in new client bookings** after implementing online scheduling.
*(Square Appointments data)*

**40% of all appointments are booked outside business hours** — evenings and weekends — when you can't answer the phone.
*(SimplyBook.me analytics, confirmed by Zippia 2026)*

**Businesses offering 24/7 online booking capture 3× more bookings** than phone-only businesses.
*(Zocdoc provider data)*

**After-hours bookings alone generate an average of $1,200/month in additional revenue** that phone-only businesses simply never see.
*(Jobber Field Service Report — Jobber serves trades and home services directly)*

**94% of consumers are more likely to choose a provider that offers online booking** — nearly universal preference.
*(GetApp Booking Preferences Survey)*

**48% of consumers have switched providers specifically because of a poor or missing booking experience.**
*(Zendesk CX Trends Report)*

**82% of consumers prefer booking online vs. by phone.** Only 33% prefer phone booking — and that number skews heavily older.
*(GetApp / Zippia 2026)*

**Online-booked appointments show up at 3.2× the rate of phone bookings** — fewer no-shows, more completed jobs.
*(Zocdoc provider insights)*

**Automated appointment reminders (built into most booking tools) cut no-shows by 29%.**
*(10to8 booking platform data, cited in Zippia 2026)*

**Saves 8 hours/week in admin time** on average — scheduling calls, callbacks, confirming times.
*(Calendly productivity report)*
